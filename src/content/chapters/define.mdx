## The myth

For years, business owners have been told that to systemise a business, they need to document everything. Every process. Every step. Every variation. Every edge case.

This is wrong.

<PullQuote id="quote-20-percent-systems">
  Just 20 per cent of the systems you create will provide 80 per cent of your efficiency wins.
</PullQuote>

## What you actually need

You need ten to fifteen systems. Not hundreds. Not thousands. Ten to fifteen.

These are the systems that move one customer through one product from awareness to repeat purchase. We call this the <Definition term="Critical Client Flow" slug="critical-client-flow">the 10-15 systems that move a customer from lead to repeat buyer</Definition>, or CCF for short.

The CCF is the heart of the SYSTEMology methodology. Get this right, and the rest of the framework falls into place.

<PullQuote id="quote-complexity-enemy">
  Complexity is the enemy of systemisation.
</PullQuote>

## The 7 stages of the CCF

1. **Attention** — how prospects first hear about you
2. **Enquiry** — how they get in touch
3. **Sales** — how they decide to buy
4. **Money** — how they pay
5. **Onboarding** — how they become an active customer
6. **Delivery** — how you deliver the core product or service
7. **Repeat or Referral** — how they buy again or send others

Walk one customer through one product through these seven stages. List the systems each stage requires. That's your CCF.

## What to do now

1. Identify ONE primary target client.
2. Identify ONE primary product or service.
3. Map the seven CCF stages on a single page.
4. List the 10-15 systems that emerge.
5. Show the completed CCF to someone outside the business — if they need it explained, simplify.

That's Stage 1. Define the Critical Client Flow. Everything in the rest of the book builds on it.
